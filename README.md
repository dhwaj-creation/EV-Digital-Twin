# 🚗 EV Digital Twin (Physics-Based Simulation)

## 📌 Overview
This project simulates an Electric Vehicle (EV) using physics-based modeling.  
It calculates power consumption, efficiency, and estimated range under different driving conditions such as city and highway driving.

---

## ⚙️ Features
- EV Power vs Speed graph
- Aerodynamic drag + rolling resistance modeling
- Driving cycle simulation (City & Highway)
- Efficiency calculation (Wh/km)
- Real-world range estimation
- Insight generation based on simulation results

---

## 🧠 Concepts Used
- Newton’s Laws of Motion
- Aerodynamics (Drag Force)
- Energy & Power Calculations
- Simulation Modeling

---

## 📊 Results Example

- City Efficiency: ~57 Wh/km  
- Highway Efficiency: ~106 Wh/km  

- City Range: ~800+ km  
- Highway Range: ~450+ km  

---

## 🏙️ Driving Conditions

### City Driving
- Stop-and-go traffic
- Lower speeds
- Better efficiency due to reduced drag

### Highway Driving
- Constant high speeds
- Higher aerodynamic drag
- Lower efficiency

---

## 🔬 Key Insight
City driving is more efficient than highway driving due to lower aerodynamic losses and higher potential for energy recovery.
